Sea Freight

The World's Oceans, At Your Service

Economical, reliable ocean freight solutions connecting East Africa's ports to every major shipping lane across the globe.

Full-Service Ocean Freight from Port to Door

Leveraging Kenya's strategic position on the Indian Ocean coast, Fresh Global Solutions provides comprehensive sea freight services through the Port of Mombasa — East Africa's largest and busiest gateway port. We manage FCL (Full Container Load) and LCL (Less than Container Load) shipments on all major trade routes, including Asia, Europe, the Middle East, and the Americas.

Our operations team coordinates vessel bookings, container stuffing and de-stuffing, port clearance, and inland transportation, delivering a seamless experience from first mile to final mile. We also offer refrigerated container solutions for temperature-sensitive cargo moving by sea.

  • Full Container Load (FCL) and LCL consolidation
  • Port of Mombasa operations and management
  • Reefer container services for perishable cargo
  • Inland container depot (ICD) management
  • Bill of lading preparation and management
  • Vessel tracking and arrival notifications
  • Multi-country routing for landlocked markets
Why Sea Freight

Benefits of Our Ocean Freight Service

Sea freight is the backbone of global trade — and our expertise makes it work harder for your business.

Most Cost-Effective Option

Sea freight is significantly cheaper per kilogram than air freight, making it the preferred choice for bulk, heavy, or non-urgent shipments.

Large Volume Capacity

Move entire production runs, machinery, or raw materials in single or multiple containers without the weight restrictions of air cargo.

Secure Containerisation

Sealed containers provide excellent protection against damage, theft, and contamination throughout the ocean voyage.

Flexible Scheduling

Regular weekly sailings on all key routes allow you to plan shipments around your production and sales cycles with confidence.

Hazardous Cargo Handling

We are certified to handle dangerous goods (IMO classes) by sea, with full compliance documentation and specialised container arrangements.
